## Introduction
Navigating the data job market can be overwhelming, with information spread across many platforms. This dashboard (v2.0) is built to simplify that process for job seekers, career switchers, and professionals exploring new opportunities. Using real-world 2024 data science job postings, it brings together key details such as job titles, salaries, and locations in one place. The project delivers a streamlined, single-page dashboard that makes it easy to explore market trends, compare roles, and understand compensation patterns at a glance.

## Skills Showcased

- **Dashboard Design:** Crafted an intuitive and visually appealing report layout focused on clarity and usability.

- **Power Query (ETL):** Performed data cleaning, shaping, and transformation to prepare raw data for analysis.

- **Data Modeling:** Built efficient data models using well-defined relationships and star schema principles.

- **DAX Fundamentals:** Created calculated measures and aggregations to derive meaningful business insights.

- **Data Visualization:**

    - **Core charts:** Column, bar, line, and area charts for trend and comparison analysis

    - **Cards:** Highlighted key performance indicators

    - **Tables:** Presented detailed, structured information

    - **Chart variety:** Selected both common and less common visuals to support effective storytelling

- **Interactivity & Navigation:**

    - **Slicers:** Enabled dynamic filtering to explore data by role, location, or other dimensions

    - **Buttons & Bookmarks:** Supported smooth navigation and guided user interactions within the dashboard


## Dashboard Overview

This second iteration consolidates the dashboard into a single, focused page, providing job seekers with the most critical market insights at a glance.


![Dashboard Page](/Images/project2_page1.png)

This page serves as a concise mission control for the data job market.
It highlights key performance indicators (KPIs) such as **job counts, skills per job, median yearly salary, and median hourly salary**.
Users can quickly assess skill popularity **by job percentage or job count** and compare salaries across different job titles, all within a single, efficient overview.
